Jambalaya

Satisfy your cravings for Southern comfort food with a heaping plate of jambalaya. This rice dish is cooked with a mix of meat, seafood, and veggies, infused with the signature flavors of Creole cuisine. A medley of spices like paprika, cayenne pepper, and thyme give it a zesty kick that's sure to please your taste buds. Follow our recipe to recreate this Louisiana classic in your own kitchen.

Ingredients:

  1. 1 pound boneless, skinless chicken breasts or thighs, cut into bite-sized pieces
  2. 1 pound andouille sausage, sliced
  3. 1 pound shrimp, peeled and deveined
  4. 1 onion, diced
  5. 1 green bell pepper, diced
  6. 3 stalks celery, diced
  7. 3 cloves garlic, minced
  8. 1 can diced tomatoes, drained
  9. 2 cups chicken broth
  10. 1 1/2 cups uncooked long-grain white rice
  11. 2 tablespoons olive oil
  12. 2 teaspoons paprika
  13. 1/2 teaspoon cayenne pepper
  14. 1/2 teaspoon dried thyme
  15. Salt and pepper, to taste
  16. Fresh parsley, chopped, for garnish

    17. Instructions:

    • 1. In a large pot or Dutch oven, heat the olive oil over medium-high heat. Add the chicken and cook until browned, about 5 minutes.
    • 2. Add the sausage to the pot and cook for another 5 minutes. Remove the chicken and sausage from the pot and set aside.
    • 3. Add the onion, bell pepper, and celery to the pot and cook until softened, about 5-7 minutes. Add the garlic and cook for an additional minute.
    • 4. Stir in the diced tomatoes, chicken broth, uncooked rice, and spices. Bring the mixture to a boil, then reduce the heat to low, cover the pot, and let it simmer for about 20 minutes, or until the rice is tender and most of the liquid has been absorbed.
    • 5. Add the cooked chicken, sausage, and shrimp to the pot and stir to combine. Cover and cook for an additional 5-10 minutes, until the shrimp are pink and cooked through.

Serve the jambalaya hot, garnished with fresh parsley. Enjoy!
