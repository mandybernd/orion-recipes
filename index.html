Odin Recipes

The best of Louisiana's taste: Creole cuisine.

Louisiana reminds me of my childhood, even though I am a Brazilian citizen. I grew up among Americans most of the time, and the most special people came from Louisiana.
Some of those memories are related to Creole cuisine. In summary, Creole cuisine of Louisiana is a blend of French, Spanish, African, and Native American culinary traditions.
It is known for its use of flavorful spices, seafood, and rice-based dishes. Some popular dishes in Creole cuisine include gumbo, jambalaya, crawfish étouffée, and red beans and rice.

Be that as it may, the recipes I will share with you today are in memory of my late uncle Donnie Shaffer, who passed away on this day, April 26th. Rest in peace, Uncle Donnie.

He was a great cook and shared many delicious recipes with our family, including some traditional Creole dishes. I hope you enjoy them as much as we did.

A collection of recipes from the south!
